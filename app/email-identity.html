What this is

This section guides you on how to use different email addresses for different purposes, especially separating your critical accounts (banking, government, primary communication) from less important online services (shopping, newsletters, forums).

Plain definition

Using multiple email addresses to compartmentalize your digital identity and minimize the impact of a single email compromise.

Why it matters

Your email address is often the key to resetting passwords and accessing many of your online accounts. If a less secure service you signed up for with your primary email gets breached, your primary email becomes vulnerable, potentially exposing all linked accounts.

Real risk explanation with concrete examples

Imagine using your main email for a new online store. That store suffers a data breach, and your email address is leaked. Now, spammers target your primary inbox, and attackers might try to use that email to reset passwords on your banking or social media accounts. By using a separate "junk" email for such services, you contain the risk to that less critical address.
