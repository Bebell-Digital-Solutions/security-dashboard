Scam and Fraud Awareness

Learn to recognize and avoid common online scams and fraud attempts.

What this is

This section educates you on various types of online scams, phishing attempts, and fraudulent schemes, providing practical tips to identify and avoid them. It focuses on developing a skeptical mindset towards unsolicited communications.

Plain definition

Understanding the tactics used by fraudsters to trick you into revealing personal information or sending money, and developing habits to protect yourself.

Why it matters

Scams and fraud are constantly evolving. Being aware of common techniques helps you protect your finances, identity, and personal data from malicious actors who exploit trust and urgency.

Real risk explanation with concrete examples

A common phishing scam involves an email that looks like it's from your bank, asking you to "verify your account" by clicking a link. If you click and enter your credentials on the fake site, attackers steal your login information. Another example is a tech support scam where a pop-up claims your computer is infected and asks you to call a number, leading to remote access and demands for payment. Recognizing these patterns is your first line of defense.

Common mistakes written as warnings

  • Warning: Assuming an email is legitimate because it looks official. Always verify the sender.
  • Warning: Acting quickly under pressure from urgent-sounding scam messages. Take a moment to think and verify.
  • Warning: Sharing personal details or clicking links without double-checking the source.
