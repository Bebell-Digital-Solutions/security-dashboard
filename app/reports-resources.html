Reports and Resources

Know where to report incidents and find additional security resources.

What this is

This section provides information on official channels for reporting cyber incidents, scams, and fraud, along with a curated list of reliable external resources for ongoing learning and support.

Plain definition

A guide to official reporting paths for security incidents and a collection of trusted external resources for further privacy and security education.

Why it matters

Knowing where to report incidents is crucial for both your own recovery and for contributing to the broader fight against cybercrime. Access to reliable resources ensures you can continue to stay informed and protect yourself.

Real risk explanation with concrete examples

If you fall victim to a phishing scam, reporting it to the relevant authorities (e.g., FTC, FBI's IC3) can help them track down criminals and prevent others from being victimized. If you discover a vulnerability, knowing where to report it responsibly can help improve overall security. Without this knowledge, incidents might go unreported, allowing threats to persist.
