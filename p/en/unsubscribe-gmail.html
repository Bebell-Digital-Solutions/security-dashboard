How to Manage and Unsubscribe from Third-Party Applications in Gmail

A comprehensive step-by-step guide to review and remove third-party app permissions connected to your Gmail account. This procedure is recommended for Google Personal Accounts.

This tutorial explains how to manage and unsubscribe from third-party applications connected to your Gmail account. Regularly reviewing these connections helps protect your security and privacy.

1 Go to www.google.com

Open your preferred web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, etc.) and navigate to the Google homepage.

Important

Make sure you are logged into your Personal Google Account. Check the profile icon in the top-right corner.

2 Access Your Google Account

Click on your profile picture or initial in the top-right corner of the Google homepage. From the dropdown menu, select "Manage your Google Account".

3 Navigate to Security Settings

You will be taken to your account management page. In the left sidebar, click on the "Security" tab.

4 Find Third-Party App Access

Scroll down the Security page until you see the section titled "Third-party apps with account access". Click on "Manage third-party access".

5 Review Connected Apps

You will now see a list of all applications and services that have permission to access some part of your Google account. This includes email clients, shopping sites, productivity tools, and more.

Tip

Pay attention to the "Last accessed" date. Apps you haven't used in years are good candidates for removal.

6 Inspect an App's Permissions

To see what an app can do, click on its name in the list. A details panel will open showing the specific permissions granted (e.g., "View your email messages and settings").

7 Remove App Access

If you want to revoke an app's access, click the blue "Remove Access" button at the bottom of the details panel.

Heads Up

Removing access might break the functionality of that app or service. You may need to sign in again if you decide to use it later.

8 Confirm Removal

A confirmation pop-up will appear. Read the warning and click "OK" to finalize the removal of the app's access to your account.

9 Repeat for Other Apps

Go through the list and remove access for any apps you no longer use, don't recognize, or that have overly broad permissions. There is no "select all" feature; you must remove them one by one.

10 Completion & Best Practices

You have successfully cleaned up your third-party app connections. For ongoing security, consider these best practices:

  • Audit Quarterly: Review this list every 3-6 months.
  • Be Selective: Only grant access to apps from trusted developers.
  • Use Strong Authentication: Ensure 2-Step Verification is enabled on your Google Account.
  • Revoke After Use: If you use a site for a one-time purpose, remove its access immediately after.

Alternative Quick Method

You can also access the third-party apps page directly from inside Gmail:

  1. While logged into Gmail, look at the URL in your browser's address bar.
  2. It will look like: https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#inbox
  3. Select the part that says u/0/#inbox and replace it with #sub.
  4. The new URL should be: https://mail.google.com/mail/#sub
  5. Press Enter. This will take you directly to your subscriptions and connected apps page.

Note: This method may not show all apps and might display primarily email subscriptions. The Security method (Steps 1-10) is more comprehensive.
